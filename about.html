Background

Why We Built This

Smallholder farmers in Tanzania face increasing climate variability. Satellite remote sensing offers an affordable way to monitor crop conditions at field scale, with no ground equipment required.

🎯 Objective

Provide a free, browser-based tool for computing and visualising key agronomic indicators across three farm seasons — Preparation, Cultivation, and Harvesting — using open satellite data.

🛰️ Remote Sensing Approach

Sentinel-2 SR imagery is cloud-masked and composited monthly. Spectral indices (NDVI, MNDWI, NDMI, CIre, NDRE) are derived per-pixel and averaged over the study farm boundary.

🌧️ Rainfall Integration

CHIRPS daily precipitation is summed per month, enabling direct comparison between rainfall inputs and vegetation response in the trend charts.

🌡️ Temperature Monitoring

MODIS MOD11A2 8-day LST composites are averaged monthly to detect heat stress periods, particularly critical during germination in the Preparation season.

📊 Monthly Trend Analysis

All indicators are computed for each of the 8 months (Nov 2025 – Jun 2026) and plotted as interactive line and bar charts with trendlines in the GEE sidebar panel.

What We Monitor

7 Satellite-Derived Indicators

Each indicator is computed monthly from multi-source satellite imagery, covering the full Nov 2025 – Jun 2026 farm season cycle.

🌿

NDVI

Vegetation Greenness & Crop Health — detects canopy density from bare soil to dense healthy crop.

💧

MNDWI

Modified Water & Wetness Index — distinguishes surface water and saturated soil from dry vegetation.

🪨

Soil Moisture

NDMI Proxy — estimates canopy water content and soil wetness; low values signal drought stress risk.

🧪

Soil Nutrient

Chlorophyll Index Red Edge (CIre) — proxy for leaf chlorophyll content, indicating nitrogen availability.

⚠️

Stressed Vegetation

NDRE — sensitive to early-stage nutrient or water stress before visible wilting begins.

🌧️

Monthly Rainfall

CHIRPS daily precipitation totals summed per month — validates rainfall-driven vegetation responses.

🌡️

Land Surface Temp

MODIS MOD11A2 monthly mean LST (°C) — identifies heat stress risk at critical growth stages.

Data Sources

Built on Trusted Open Datasets

Open satellite data from ESA Copernicus, UCSB, and NASA — accessed and processed via Google Earth Engine.

🛰️

Sentinel-2 SR Harmonised

COPERNICUS/S2_SR_HARMONIZED · 10–20 m spatial resolution, cloud-masked at 80% threshold for rainy-season months

🌧️

CHIRPS Daily Rainfall

UCSB-CHG/CHIRPS/DAILY · Satellite-gauge blended product at ~5.5 km, summed to monthly totals

🌡️

MODIS LST 8-day

MODIS/061/MOD11A2 · Daytime land surface temperature at 1 km resolution, averaged to monthly means

Study Area

Shamba Farm, Tanzania

The monitoring system is centred on a defined farm polygon (SHAMBA) digitised in Google Earth Engine, located in Tanzania. All spatial analyses are clipped to this boundary.
